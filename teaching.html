EE16A

I've been a GSI (UC Berkeley's term for Teaching Assistant) for the introductory Electrical Engineering course since fall of 2016, in a variety of positions including teaching discussions, managing the course Piazza, and managing our team of readers who grade homework assignments and exams.

I've written some exam questions in the past, which can be found here. My questions tend to focus on core concepts rather than intensive design or proofs.

E98

As an E98 instructor, I (with two other co-instructors) led a section of 20 freshman engineering students, structured to be a casual lecture setting encouraging question and answers. The course was one of UC Berkeley's many decal courses.

We advised them on many relevant topics including time management, writing resumes, interview attire and behavior, and choosing engineering classes. The course culminated in each student showcasing their oral communication skills by giving a short presentation on a chosen engineering topic (bitcoin, Bart transbay tunnel, DSLR cameras, etc.)

CSM

CSM (Computer Science Mentors) is a student-run organization that provides guidance and resources in a smaller classroom environment through group tutoring sessions. There are 4-6 students in each group, and we emphasize a non-judgemental learning environment. I work as a Content Mentor, so in addition to leading a section, I create additional content like worksheets and videos for students to use. View CSM's website here.

Lab Assisting

I volunteered as a lab assistant for two semesters after I finished Berkeley's introductory computer science course. This involved assisting in the weekly labs for CS61A, and also helping at course office hours for 1-2 hours per week (more during project weeks).
